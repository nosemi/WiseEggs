I have collected the recipes I use when cooking eggs! Eggs are fun and very versatile when it comes to dishes. Some are well known and some may be new. For the most part, the egg will be the main component of the dish, and I will stay away from desserts that are based on egg but are not egg, for example flan, angel food cake, or macaroons--these desserts rely heavily on egg white.

Eggs Benedict

Serves: 4        Time: 25 min. (including prep and cook time)

Ingredients:
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tbsp parsely, chopped
  • 2 English muffins, halved
  • Hollandaise sauce
  • 4-8 pieces bacon or smoked salmon (optional)
Method
  1. Cook bacon if using. Set on paper towel to absorb excess oil
  2. Boil water and poach eggs (see recipe).
  3. Toast English muffins. You will only need half for each serving.
  4. Assemble by putting bacon or salmon (optional) on muffin halves and then place a poached egg on top.
  5. Pour Hollandaise sauce over the egg. Garnish with chopped parsely.
  6. Serve and enjoy!

Adapted from Simply Recipes

Egg in Bread

Serves: 8        Time: 35 min. (including prep and cook time)

Ingredients:
  • 4 large eggs
  • 8 dinner rolls
  • 1/4 cup chopped herbs of choice - recommended are parsely, chives, and tarragon
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: Parmesan cheese
Method
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Slice off the tops of the dinner rolls and carefully remove the inside breading so that the egg will not spill over. Place tops aside.
  2. Crack egg into each emptied dinner roll and season with herb mix, salt, and pepper. If choosing to use cheese, sprinkle some parmesan over cracked eggs now.
  3. Place dinner rolls into oven and bake for 20 to 25 minutes. Once the eggs seem set, place the sliced tops onto the rolls and bake for another 5 minutes or so. They should be golden brown.
  4. Take out of oven and let rest for a couple minutes.
  5. Serve and enjoy!

Century Egg

Serves: Varies        Time: 4-5 weeks

Ingredients:
  • Egg(s)
  • Salt
  • 100% Lye or Caustic soda
Materials:
  • Scale
  • Plastic wrap
  • Clay - modeling clay works
  • Glass jar with lid
Method
  1. Prepare a pickling solution by combining 1L water, 42g lye, and 72g salt. Make sure to measure out the precise measurements. Bring to a boil and allow to cool.
  2. Place the raw eggs in glass jars and pour the pickling solution over eggs so that they are completely submerged.
  3. Leave eggs for about 10 days and keep in cool temperature, around 60-70°F. Make sure they stay submerged.
  4. After the 10 days, take out eggs, rinse them, and dry using a towel. The shells may looked slightly discolored and this is expected.
  5. Wrap the eggs in layers of plastic wrap and then mold the clay around it so it is completely concealed. Be careful as to not break the egg shells. Place in a sealable bag for 2 weeks.
  6. After the 2 weeks or so, remove the clay and peel away the plastic wrap. Carefully break the egg shell. The egg should appear much darker.
  7. Add to a bowl of rice, which is how they are typically eaten.
  8. Serve and enjoy!

Deviled Egg

Serves: 6        Time: 40 min.

Ingredients:
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Pinch cayenne pepper
  • Chopped chives
  • Optional: Paprika
Method
  1. Place eggs in saucepan and cover with water. Bring to a boil and cook for 5 minutes. Take off heat and let rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Transfer eggs to a bowl of ice and cold water for 5 minutes to stop the cooking process.
  3. Peel eggs and cut longitudinal. Remove yolks but keep for later.
  4. Make the filling. Mix yolks, mayonnaise, mustard, and cayenne and mix with a fork until smooth. Scoop into a pastry bag and carefully pipe into each egg white half.
  5. Top with paprika and chives.
  6. Serve and enjoy!

Egg Drop Soup

Serves: 6 cups        Time: 10 min.

Ingredients:
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg whites
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • Pinch garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup green onion, sliced thin
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: 1/4 cup corn
Method
  1. Mix together chicken stock, cornstarch, ginger powder, and garlic powder in a saucepan until there are no lumps present.
  2. Bring to boil with occasional stirring. Remove from heat once broth reaches boil.
  3. Whisk eggs and egg whites in a small bowl. While stirring or gently whisking the broth, pour the eggs into the hot broth.
  4. Stir in sesame oil, green onion, and optional corn to taste. Garnish with green onion.
  5. Serve and enjoy!

Eggnog

Serves: 18        Time: 45 min. (including prep and cook time)

Ingredients:
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 oz. dark rum
  • 2 oz. bourbon
  • 1 oz. cognac
  • 3/4 cup sugar - can substitute with Stevia or another sweetener
  • Freshly grated nutmeg
  • Optional: Whipped cream
Method
  1. Whisk egg yolks and 1/2 cup sugar in heatproof bowl until pale yellow and thick. Add milk and salt to a medium saucepan and bring to a slow simmer. Whisking constantly, pour hot milk mixture into egg yolks.
  2. Pour egg yolks and milk mixture back into saucepan and cook over medium heat until the mixture is thick, about 3-5 minutes.
  3. Pour mixture through a fine-mesh sieve and discard of the solids. Allow to cool for 20 minutes while stirring. Add cream, bourbon, cognac, and rum. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  4. Whisk egg whites and remaining 1/4 cup sugar and heat over a pan of simmering water. Whisk for about 2-3 minutes until sugar is dissolved. Beat with high-speed mixer until stiff peaks form and fold into eggnog.
  5. Serve in glasses and enjoy! Top with optional whipped cream.

Poached Egg

Serves: Depends        Time: 5 min. (without boil time)

Ingredients:
  • Egg(s) - amount can range depending on pot size
  • Water - fill up to about 2/3 line
  • 1 tbsp Rice Vinegar - or any other tasteless vinegar
Method
  1. Boil the water, and once boiled, add the vinegar.
  2. Bring heat to low medium until the boil becomes a low simmer. There should still be bubbles but not as strong as when boiling.
  3. Take a spoon and swirl the water.
  4. Crack or drop the egg into the swirl and watch as the egg white enclose the yolk. I prefer to crack my eggs into a somewhat shallow bowl (I use a 1/3 cup spoon) and then gently drop it into the water.
  5. Cook the eggs for about 3 minutes and 40 seconds for a runny yolk. If you'd prefer a firmer yolk, cook for about 30 seconds to a minute more.
  6. Take out the poached egg carefully with a slotted spoon and try to get any excess water off.
  7. Serve and enjoy!

From here, you can add the poached egg on toast, oatmeal (a personal favorite), or simply have it plain. It's all about what you like!
